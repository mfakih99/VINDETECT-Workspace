VIN Fraud Typology

Ghost VINs

Completely fabricated 17-character identifiers that appear structurally valid but never corresponded to any legitimately manufactured vehicle.

• Synthetically created from scratch
• Pass basic format validation
• Used for "paper vehicles" in insurance scams

Cloned VINs (Re-VINning)

Legitimate VINs stolen from registered vehicles and applied to stolen or salvaged vehicles with counterfeit VIN plates.

• Two vehicles sharing same VIN
• Often similar make/model/year
• Creates significant confusion and risk

Synthetic VINs

Artificially generated VINs with comprehensive false identities, including forged titles and counterfeit Manufacturer Certificates of Origin.

• Elaborate deception measures
• False documentation packages
• Exploit verification system gaps

Current Detection Limitations

NMVTIS Gaps

  • • "No record found" for ghost VINs not flagged as fraudulent
  • • Reactive nature - tracks known VINs only
  • • Cannot determine authenticity of plausible VINs

Traditional Methods

  • • Checksum validation easily bypassed by fraudsters
  • • Visual inspection defeated by high-quality counterfeits
  • • Focus on known "bad" VINs vs. authenticity verification

Understanding VIN Standards

Federal regulation 49 CFR Part 565 establishes the comprehensive framework for Vehicle Identification Numbers, creating the foundation for our advanced fraud detection capabilities.

17-Character VIN Structure

Positions 1-3

World Manufacturer Identifier (WMI)

• Identifies manufacturer and country of origin

• Assigned by Society of Automotive Engineers (SAE)

• Critical for authenticity validation

Positions 4-8

Vehicle Descriptor Section (VDS)

• Model, body style, engine type details

• Manufacturer-specific encoding

• Submitted to NHTSA for verification

Positions 9-17

Vehicle Identifier Section (VIS)

• Check digit (position 9) for validation

• Model year (position 10) and plant code (11)

• Sequential production number (12-17)

Character Set Restrictions

Permitted Characters:

• Capital letters A-Z

• Numbers 0-9

• Total: 33 permissible characters

Excluded Characters:

• Letter I (confusion with numeral 1)

• Letter O (confusion with numeral 0)

• Letter Q (additional clarity measure)

Federal Criminal Law

18 U.S.C. §511 criminalizes VIN fraud:

  • Knowing alteration of VINs
  • Forging vehicle identifiers
  • Removal of VIN plates
  • Penalties: Up to 5 years imprisonment

Check Digit Algorithm

Position 9 validation through mathematical calculation:

  • Weighted sum of characters 1-8, 10-17
  • Modulo 11 operation for verification
  • Public algorithm exploited by fraudsters
  • Passes basic checks but not authenticity
